# 01 — Business Case & Market Research

## Executive Summary

"Don't Quote Me" attacks the single biggest unpaid bottleneck in Australian trades: the quoting workflow. Tradespeople lose 10–20+ hours/week on non-billable admin. Median conversion is ~38% (62% wasted effort). Quotes that don’t convert sit untouched for a median 29 days. In a market facing acute labour shortages and a push for 1.2 million new homes, fast, accurate quoting is a competitive superpower.

The global construction estimating software market is growing from ~USD 1.5B (2024) to USD 2.62B by 2030 at 10.2% CAGR. Australia alone has 100,000+ tradies and small builders who quote constantly.

"Don't Quote Me" is not another SaaS app — it is **Service-as-Software**: the tradie texts their quoting assistant (WhatsApp/Telegram) and receives a finished, professional, priced quote with BOM, real-time Bunnings pricing, and 3D sketch.

## The Problem — Deep Dive

### Time-Consuming & Labour-Intensive
- Quoting consumes ~51% of admin time (Tradify UK/AU data)
- Specialists spend ~16 hours/week (UK) or up to ~19 hours/week (AU) on non-billable admin
- Anecdotes: 2–3 hours/day just on quotes; up to 2 weeks for complex estimates
- 17% of businesses cite manual data entry as top CRM/quoting challenge
- Site visits, measurements, photos, follow-ups — often for jobs that never convert

### Low Conversion Rates & Time Wasters
- Median conversion: ~38% overall (62% of quoting effort wasted)
- Even filtered to decided quotes: 60–74% conversion
- 432k+ quote dataset: non-converting quotes sit untouched for median 29 days
- Australian builders report "time-consuming quotes with low conversion" after days on unprepared clients
- Reddit/Facebook forums: tradies quoting 45 jobs/month but winning just 5

### Inaccuracy, Volatility, Margin Erosion
- Material prices (timber, steel) fluctuate weekly due to supply chains and inflation
- Fixed-price quotes locked months ahead become loss-makers
- Manual BOMs and estimates cause under-quoting (lost profit) or over-quoting (lost jobs)
- Bad data cost the global industry $1.8 trillion in 2020 ($88B in avoidable rework)
- Australia: +39.7% cost rise post-pandemic, universal labour shortages, longer build times

### Anecdotal Realities
- Tradespeople: "Estimates are a huge pain point… it can take 2 weeks."
- Clients: Frustration with delays, no-shows, vague quotes → "get 3 quotes" advice multiplies the pain
- Broader impact: Quotes eat billable time, limiting growth and damaging trust

## Market Opportunity

### Australia-Specific
- $1.38T in forecasted building work over 5 years
- National housing target: 1.2 million new homes
- Universal skilled labour shortages across all construction occupations
- Master Builders Australia: workforce shortages are a core barrier to housing targets

### Global
- Construction estimating software: USD 1.5B (2024) → USD 2.62B (2030) at 10.2% CAGR
- Many tradies still rely on spreadsheets/Word — massive software adoption gap
- Bunnings already offers partner APIs for pricing/stock integration

### Proven Demand for Outsourced/Done-for-You Estimating
- Existing firms (World Estimating, Total Takeoffs, iAM Builders, AAA Estimating) offer pay-per-estimate or monthly packages
- Pricing: $100–$500+ per estimate for smaller jobs; monthly retainers ~$1,500 for volume
- This proves tradespeople already pay for the outcome, not the tool

## The Solution — Service as a Software

### Core Promise
"Send us the job details. We’ll return a ready-to-send, priced, professional quote in under 30–60 minutes — no app to learn, no measurements to take yourself."

### What Makes It Different from SaaS
| Traditional SaaS | Service as a Software |
|---|---|
| Sells the tool (app, dashboard) | Sells the outcome (finished quote) |
| User must learn and operate | Zero learning curve |
| Captures $1 (software) | Captures $1 + $6 (software + labour replaced) |
| User does the work | AI does the work; user just confirms |

### Quantifiable Benefits
| Benefit | Mechanism | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Time Savings | Slash quoting from hours/days to minutes | Reclaim 10–20+ admin hours/week |
| Higher Conversion | Same-day professional quotes reduce "stale quote" losses | 10–20% lift (38% → 48–58%) |
| Better Margins | Real-time pricing + accurate quantities | Fewer under/overquotes |
| Scalability | Handle more leads without extra staff | More jobs completed |

### ROI Example
A solo tradie spending 15–20 hours/week quoting could redirect that to billable work. At typical margins (2–5% net in construction), payback is rapid. Reduced bad-data losses compound gains.

## Risks & Mitigations (High-Level)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Adoption friction | WhatsApp/Telegram native — zero new app |
| Accuracy concerns | Smart defaults + flagged assumptions; human review for >$5k |
| Data privacy | AU data residency, auto-delete media after processing, row-level security |
| Price volatility | "Prices current as of [time]" disclaimers; provisional sums |
| Competition | Narrow focus on chat-native, done-for-you outcome; personal RAG flywheel |

## Conclusion & Next Steps

The quoting problem is real, well-documented, and expensive. "Don't Quote Me" attacks the root cause with a practical, on-site-first tool that delivers measurable efficiency, higher conversions, and stronger profitability.

Recommended next actions:
1. Validate with quick user interviews (1–2 trades)
2. Build MVP focused on one trade (electricians or plumbers, Sydney metro)
3. Launch with first 10 quotes heavily discounted to seed RAG profiles
4. Iterate based on real-world quote outcomes
