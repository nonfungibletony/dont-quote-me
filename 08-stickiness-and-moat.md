# 08 — Stickiness, Moat & Retention Strategy

## The Moat: Compound Flywheel

The system prompt is zero moat — anyone can copy-paste it. The real defensibility is a **compound flywheel** that generic AI can't touch:

| Moat Layer | Why It's Hard to Copy | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| **Proprietary Per-Tradie Data** | Every quote, assumption, tweak, and outcome becomes private RAG knowledge | **High** — builds over time per business |
| **Workflow Embedding** | Lives inside WhatsApp/Telegram (where tradies already live) | **Very High** — zero switching friction |
| **Real-Time Integrations** | Bunnings + other AU supplier APIs + live pricing | **Medium-High** — requires partnerships |
| **Human Safety Net + Trust** | Hybrid AI + optional human review for big jobs | **High** — tradies won't trust pure AI on $10k+ quotes |
| **Community & Brand** | "The quoting assistant Australian tradies actually use" | **Medium** — grows via referrals |

## Why a Simple Prompt Won't Kill This Business

A lone tradie prompting Claude or GPT will get:
- A **one-off quote** with no memory
- No supplier pricing, no 3D sketch, no branded PDF
- No history, no flywheel
- No trust: "I used ChatGPT" doesn't win jobs

Your edge is turning the service into an **invisible virtual employee whose performance compounds with every quote**.

## Stickiness Engine: Personalised Data Flywheel

### How It Works

1. **Every quote is saved** (with permission) as private RAG context
2. **The agent gets smarter for that specific tradie:**
   - "You always add 15% contingency on renos — shall I include it?"
   - "Last three kitchens you quoted used premium tapware — keeping that default?"
   - Auto-flags recurring scope creep they've missed before
3. **Result:** After 20–30 quotes, it feels like it knows their business better than they do

### Onboarding as Data Seeding
- **First 3 quotes free or heavily discounted** — immediate value + data seeding
- Agent asks during onboarding: "Tell me your usual margins, favourite suppliers, common add-ons"
- This builds the initial RAG profile that improves every future quote

### Daily Chat Flow Intelligence
- `/new` → instant thread
- Burst input still works, but now the agent says:
  - *"Compared to your last 3 similar jobs, this one looks 12% higher on labour — intentional?"*
- One-tap "Apply my usual rules" button

### Post-Quote Intelligence (The Lock-In Layer)
- After customer accepts/rejects: Tradie forwards customer reply into the thread
- Agent logs win/loss + reason
- **Monthly private report:**
  - "Your win rate this month: 68% (industry avg 38%)."
  - "Here's why you lost the other 32% + how to fix."
- **Quote Insights tab:** searchable history, average margins by trade type, common customer objections

## Pricing That Rewards Volume & Creates Sunk Cost

| Tier | Pricing | Psychology |
|---|---|---|
| First 10 quotes/month | $29 each | Low barrier to try |
| 11–50 quotes/month | $19 each | Volume discount locks in regulars |
| 51+ quotes/month | $14 each | Heavy users feel "insider" pricing |
| Unlimited | $149–$249/month | "I'm getting a deal" + predictable cost |

**Additional lock-in mechanics:**
- Quote credits **never expire** and roll over — creates sunk-cost feel
- Referral: Both tradie and referee get 5 free quotes — network effect
- Annual plan: 2 months free — long-term commitment

## Deeper Integrations (Future but Powerful)

1. **Accounting Export**
   - One-click export to Xero/MYOB
   - Optional: Auto-create job in their existing system once quote is accepted

2. **Anonymised Benchmarks**
   - "Tradies in Sydney with similar job mix are winning 8% more by adding provisional sums here."
   - Value-add insight that keeps them engaged

3. **Supplier Loyalty Programs**
   - "You've ordered $5,000 through Bunnings this quarter — here's a 5% discount code."
   - Creates shared value between tradie, supplier, and platform

## Emotional & Experience Stickiness

| Tactic | How |
|---|---|
| **Speed + Delight** | First draft in <45 seconds. Revisions feel like texting your best apprentice. |
| **Emotional Hook** | Agent remembers personal details: "How's the new ute going?" or "Congrats on landing that big reno last week." |
| **Risk Reversal** | "If this quote loses you the job due to our error, we refund the quote fee + $100 goodwill." |
| **Community Moat** | Private tradie-only group (or Slack) where users share tips. Best ideas feed back into the agent. |

## Tokenising Quotes (Not Blockchain)

Don't tokenise in the blockchain sense — that adds zero value for tradies.

Instead, **treat every quote as a data token internally**:
- Each quote improves the model for that user
- Anonymised patterns improve the model for similar users
- This is the real "tokenisation" — turning quotes into **compounding intelligence**

## Switching Cost Calculation

**What a tradie loses if they leave:**
- All quote history + learned rules (irreplaceable)
- Personalised accuracy that took 20–30 quotes to build
- Monthly win/loss insights and benchmarking
- Workflow embedded in the chat they use every day
- Supplier relationships and discount codes

**Result:** After 3 months of regular use, switching cost is **massive**.

## Retention Metrics & Targets

| Metric | Month 1 | Month 3 | Month 6 | Month 12 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Activation (first quote) | 80% | — | — | — |
| Repeat quote (2nd quote) | 60% | 70% | 75% | 80% |
| Monthly active rate | 50% | 60% | 70% | 75% |
| Churn (monthly) | 15% | 10% | 8% | 5% |
| NPS | 30 | 40 | 45 | 50 |

## Anti-Churn Interventions

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| No quote in 14 days | "Miss us? Here's a free quote credit — try our new [feature]." |
| No quote in 30 days | Personal outreach: "What would get you back? Feedback call?" |
| Win rate dropping | Automated tip: "Your win rate dropped 10% — here's what's working for similar tradies." |
| Competitor mention | "We noticed you tried [competitor]. Here's what we do differently." |
| Plan downgrade | "Switch to pay-per-quote? Keep your history and come back anytime." |
