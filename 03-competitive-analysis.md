# 03 — Competitive Analysis

## Competitor Landscape (2026)

### 1. Full-Featured Job Management Platforms (AU-Focused)

| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses vs. Don't Quote Me | Threat Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tradify** | Strong AU presence, quoting + scheduling + invoicing | Still requires learning app; quoting is a feature, not the core service | Medium |
| **Buildxact** | "Blu" AI assistant, live dealer pricing, takeoff reviewer | Bloated all-in-one; on-site capture clunky | **High** |
| **ServiceM8** | Good for field service, mobile-first | Quoting module requires manual input | Medium |
| **SimPRO** | Enterprise-grade for larger trade businesses | Complex, expensive, not solo-tradie friendly | Low |
| **Fergus** | Plumbing-focused, good job tracking | Desktop-heavy, not chat-native | Low |

**Key insight:** These platforms dominate because they do everything. But "everything" means complexity. Solo tradies and small teams often use only 20% of features. None offer a pure chat-native, done-for-you quoting experience.

### 2. Pure AI Estimating / Takeoff Tools

| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses vs. Don't Quote Me | Threat Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Togal.AI** | Strong plan takeoffs, 90–98% accuracy | Desktop/web-first; leaves formatting/pricing to user | Medium |
| **STACK** | Good for blueprint analysis | Subscription-heavy ($149–$299+/mo); not chat-native | Medium |
| **Handoff.AI** | Photo analysis for residential/remodel | No conversational fine-tuning; no WhatsApp integration | Medium |
| **QuoteIQ** | Emerging AI quoting | Partial solution; no end-to-end outcome | Medium |
| **Beam** | Plan-based estimating | No on-site chat workflow | Low |

**Key insight:** These tools solve part of the problem (takeoff/estimating) but don't deliver the "done-for-you outcome" in a chat the tradie already uses. They are **features**, not a **service**.

### 3. Outsourced Estimating Services (Human + Hybrid)

| Competitor | Pricing | Speed | Weakness vs. Don't Quote Me |
|---|---|---|---|
| **World Estimating** | $100–$500+/estimate | 24–48 hrs | Slow, expensive, no iteration |
| **Total Takeoffs** | Monthly retainers ~$1,500 | 24–48 hrs | No on-site immediacy |
| **iAM Builders** | Pay-per-estimate | 24–48 hrs | No 3D sketch, no chat |
| **AAA Estimating** | Varies | 24–48 hrs | No real-time supplier pricing |
| **Real Estimate Services** | Varies | 24–48 hrs | No AI-powered speed |
| **Melt Takeoff** (AI-augmented) | Varies | Faster than pure human | Still not instant; no chat workflow |

**Key insight:** These prove demand for outsourced quoting. "Don't Quote Me" is the AI-native, instant version — cheaper, faster, always available.

### 4. Emerging Chat / WhatsApp Experiments

- A few Telegram/WhatsApp bots exist (n8n templates, small UK experiments)
- One UK experiment claims £1.5M in quotes generated via WhatsApp bot
- **None** appear scaled with:
  - Real-time supplier APIs
  - Personalised RAG per tradie
  - Branded PDFs
  - Human safety net
  - 3D output
  - Australian market focus

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- **Zero adoption friction** — tradies already live in WhatsApp/Telegram
- **Service-as-Software** model captures full value (software + labour replaced)
- **Personalised RAG** creates compounding intelligence per tradie
- **Narrow focus** on quoting excellence vs. bloated all-in-one platforms
- **Chat-native UX** feels like texting an apprentice, not using software
- **Speed** — first draft in <60 seconds vs. 24–48 hrs for human services

### Weaknesses
- **Accuracy risk** — AI can hallucinate quantities or miss scope
- **Liability** — quotes are semi-binding; errors cost tradies money
- **Single-purpose** — doesn't do scheduling, invoicing, job management
- **New brand** — no existing trust or market presence
- **Dependency on chat platforms** — WhatsApp/Telegram API changes could disrupt

### Opportunities
- **Massive AU market** — 100,000+ tradies, acute labour shortages
- **Proven outsourcing demand** — existing firms validate willingness to pay
- **AI maturity** — 2026 multimodal LLMs are production-ready
- **White-label** — larger trade businesses may want branded version
- **Data moat** — every quote improves the model; flywheel effect
- **Expansion** — auto-ordering, job management integrations, other countries

### Threats
- **Feature creep by incumbents** — Buildxact/Tradify could add WhatsApp bot wrapper
- **New AI-native entrants** — low barrier to entry for basic chat bot
- **Price volatility** — material costs change faster than APIs can update
- **Regulatory** — GST, consumer law, liability on AI-generated quotes
- **Trust gap** — tradies may prefer human estimators for nuanced jobs
- **Signal/quality issues** — blurry photos, unclear voice notes degrade results

## Pressure-Test Verdict

**Is this just a feature a big player can add?**

- **Yes, partially** — Buildxact or Tradify could bolt on a WhatsApp agent
- **But unlikely to kill us quickly:**
  - Big platforms move slowly on narrow, delightful UX
  - They protect all-in-one revenue; adding a chat bot risks cannibalising core product
  - Personalised per-tradie RAG is hard to replicate without starting from scratch
  - Tradies who hate learning new software will prefer pure service over "yet another module"

**Differentiation that survives:**
1. **Own the chat layer** — be "the WhatsApp quoting assistant every tradie uses"
2. **Double down on personalisation** — private RAG creates real lock-in
3. **Hybrid human tier** — for $5k+ jobs, builds trust no pure-AI tool has
4. **Data flywheel speed** — seed profiles quickly with discounted first quotes
5. **Supplier partnerships** — deep Bunnings integration = pricing moat
6. **Resist feature bloat** — stay laser-focused on quoting excellence
